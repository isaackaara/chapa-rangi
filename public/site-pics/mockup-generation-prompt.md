## STYLE DIRECTION

**Inspired by** Atlas & Moon Studio (@atlas_and_moon on Instagram) — we borrow their *techniques* (flat-lay photography, embossed paper textures, overhead angles, natural props, premium tactile quality). But the *colour palette and mood* must match chaparangi.com's own visual identity so the mockups feel native to the site, not imported from a different brand world.

### Chaparangi.com's visual identity (the palette to use):
- **Page background:** Warm cream/off-white (#F5F0EB)
- **Primary accent:** Dark teal (#1C3D3B) — used for section backgrounds, footer, badges
- **Secondary accent:** Soft coral/pink (#E8436D) — CTAs, the "i" in "chapa rangi", labels
- **Typography:** Elegant serif headings (warm, editorial), clean sans-serif body
- **Section dividers:** Organic wavy shapes between cream and teal sections
- **Overall mood:** Light, warm, airy, feminine, approachable — not dark or moody

### How the mockups should feel on-site:
- **Dominant surfaces:** Warm cream linen, light natural wood, white marble, pale stone, soft cotton — keep things LIGHT. The mockups will sit on a cream (#F5F0EB) page background, so they need to harmonise, not contrast harshly.
- **Accent surfaces (used sparingly):** Dark teal (#1C3D3B) card stock for one or two emboss shots per project — this ties to the teal sections on the website. But teal should be the exception, not the rule.
- **Lighting:** Soft, warm, natural daylight — bright and airy, not dramatic or moody. Light coming from top-left. Think "morning sun on a linen tablecloth."
- **Shadows:** Gentle, diffused. No harsh drop shadows.
- **Paper textures:** Visible grain and tooth, but on lighter materials — cream cotton card stock, natural kraft, linen-pressed paper in warm whites and beiges.
- **Props:** Dried botanicals (eucalyptus, wheat, lavender), natural linen fabric, light wood surfaces, simple ceramics. Everything warm-toned. No dark/dramatic props.
- **Composition:** Clean, intentional spacing. Designs placed slightly off-centre or at gentle angles (2-5 degrees). Generous negative space — let the designs breathe.

### Techniques borrowed from Atlas & Moon:
- Flat-lay overhead photography style
- Embossed/letterpress logo effects on textured card stock
- Stationery suite flat-lays (business card + letterhead + envelope)
- Realistic printed-piece mockups (the designs should look like they've been physically printed on real paper)
- Natural, organic prop styling
- Premium, tactile quality — everything should feel real, not digitally rendered

### What NOT to do:
- Do NOT use dark moody backgrounds as the default (Atlas & Moon's forest green, charcoal, terracotta)
- Do NOT make the overall composition dark or heavy
- Do NOT use gold/copper foil effects (that's Atlas & Moon's brand, not Chapa Rangi's)
- Do NOT use cold lighting or stark white studio backgrounds

**Aspect ratio:** 4:5 (portrait, 1600×2000px) for individual mockups. 1:1 (1600×1600px) for grid/collection shots.

- [ ] Visible paper/material texture (grain, tooth, weave) — but on LIGHT surfaces
- [ ] Soft, diffused shadows (not harsh drop shadows)
- [ ] Predominantly light palette: cream, warm whites, natural linen, light wood — with teal (#1C3D3B) as an occasional accent
- [ ] Overhead/flat-lay camera angle
- [ ] The actual design content from the source SVG is clearly visible and legible
